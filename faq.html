What is NormWise?▼
NormWise is an AI-powered compliance co-pilot for ISO management systems. It helps quality managers, ISO consultants, and QHSE directors get instant, clause-referenced answers about ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and ISO 45001 — plus perform document gap analyses and generate professional reports.
Which ISO standards does NormWise cover?▼
NormWise covers:
  • ISO 9001:2015 — Quality Management Systems (incl. Amendment 1:2024)
  • ISO 14001:2015 — Environmental Management Systems
  • ISO 45001:2018 — Occupational Health & Safety
  • ISO 9001:2026 draft — DIS approved December 2025
  • ISO 14001:2026 draft — FDIS stage
  • ISO 45001:2027 draft — Committee Draft stage
What languages does NormWise support?▼
NormWise auto-detects your language and responds 100% in English, German, or French — including all generated reports and documents.
Is NormWise a certified ISO auditing service?▼
No. NormWise is an informational tool powered by AI. It provides guidance based on ISO standards but does not replace a certified auditor or official certification body. Always verify compliance decisions with a certified ISO auditor such as TÜV SÜD, DNV, or Bureau Veritas.

features
What is the Gap Analysis feature?▼
Available on the Expert plan. You upload your quality/environmental/safety documentation and NormWise analyses it clause-by-clause against the relevant ISO standard. You receive findings classified as:
  • 🔴 Major NC — critical non-conformity requiring immediate action
  • 🟡 Minor NC — partial compliance requiring improvement
  • 🟠 OFI — opportunity for improvement
  • 🟢 Compliant — evidence of conformance found
Can NormWise generate Word and Excel files?▼
Yes — on the Professional and Expert plans. You can generate:
  • Word (.docx) Action Plans with corrective actions
  • Excel (.xlsx) Gap Analysis tables
  • PDF Management Summaries
All in your selected language (EN/DE/FR).
Does NormWise know about ISO 9001:2026?▼
Yes. NormWise has full knowledge of the ISO 9001:2026 DIS (Draft International Standard), approved December 2025 with publication expected September 2026. Key changes include new climate change integration, quality culture requirements (Clause 5), and Annex A guidance (first ever for ISO 9001).
